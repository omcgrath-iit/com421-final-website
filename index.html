Received a ticket you didn’t deserve or can’t pay?

If you received a parking ticket, you might not have to pay it. It is your right to contest your ticket in court. Over 57% of people who contest their ticket are found not liable and do not have to pay. On this page, find statistics and resources to help you appeal your ticket.

Statistics

From 2004 to 2018, the City of Chicago has issued over 12,000 parking violations in Bridgeport. Of these violations, only about 1,165 (9%) were challenged in court. In 58% of cases, the courts found that recipients of these violations were not liable and did not have to pay any money to the city.

Appealing your ticket

You must contest your ticket within 7 days of it being issued or withing 21 days of the Notice of Violation. If you wait longer than this, you will be found liable and must pay the ticket and associated fees and pentalties.

Request your hearing

You have three options to request an in-person hearing:

Online
Go to the city’s Submit a Hearing Request Online page and follow the instructions.
Over the phone
Call the City of Chicago’s Ticket Helpline, (312) 744-7275. For TTY, call (312) 744-7277.
By mail
Fill in the “request for in-person hearing” or “in-person hearing” bubble on ticket envelope or Notice of Violation. Use the mailing instructions on the ticket or Notice of Violation.
